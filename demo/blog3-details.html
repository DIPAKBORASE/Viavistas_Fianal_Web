The Importance of Visual Elements in Your Brand Strategy

When developing your brand, make sure to include a visual strategy along with the development of your story, personality, core message, and brand promise. Let's face it, no matter how good your business or messaging is, visuals are usually the most effective way to capture your audience's attention and build brand recognition, assuming you use them correctly. By incorporating visuals into your overall marketing strategy, you will help your brand's long-term success. There's no denying that today's marketing world is becoming more visual, and you'll need to be able to strategically adapt to that. Let's take a closer look.

  • What is visual brand identity and why is it so important?

    .Your visual identity includes the visual components of your brand identity, which includes all of the moving pieces that together represent how your brand is perceived. More than just colors and design go into how you present yourself visually. Brands that are consistent with their visual identity connect with their audience more effectively than those that are not. Your visual identity is a direct reflection of your company, so take the time and do the research to ensure that it is accurately and positively represented, and that it is consistent with your spoken and written messaging.

    • The importance of knowing your audience

    .Understanding who your audience is and who you're trying to attract is the foundation of your visual strategy. If you don't understand this, your visual brand, as well as your marketing and operational efforts in general, will be meaningless. Keep in mind that your brand's visual aspects are not for you. They're there for your customers, so keep that in mind at all times. Understanding their wants and needs will help you figure out how you should approach them.

  • How to make your visual brand identity stand out

    .Be distinctive – it should stand out from the crowd. To be truly effective, you must first have a thorough understanding of who your competitors are and what their visual brand identity looks like.
    .Be memorable – Aim to make your visual brand so strong that even if your audience doesn't know what they're looking at, they'll know exactly what they're looking at.
    .Make everything match – Every aspect of your visual brand should be consistent and seamless.

  • Things to consider for creating a strong visual brand identity

    .Get your logo right
    .it should stand out from the crowd. To be truly effective, you must first have a thorough understanding of who your competitors are and what their visual brand identity looks like.
    .Create a consistent color palette
    When you think of well-known brands, you'll notice that they all have similar color palettes (who doesn't think of Coca-Cola when they think of red?). These well-known brands use the same colors throughout their texts, images and designs to help them stand out. Choose only a few colors and use them in all of your projects. Colors should reflect your brand's personality, so if you run a daycare, for example, you might want to use bright colors. If you run a law firm, you might want to stick to more conservative color schemes.
    .Choose a font that matches your brand personality
    Along with your color palette, the font you use should be considered, as it can reveal a lot about the type of business you run. If you run a serious business, for example, you should avoid Comic Sans; however, if your company is light-hearted and fun, and that's how you want to be perceived, Comic Sans could be the right font for you. You must ensure that your personality matches your audience's perception of you and that it is acceptable to them.
    .Choose images that address your audience and reflect your brand
    This should go without saying, brands use images on their websites that, quite frankly, make their services more confusing than if they didn't use any at all. It's fine to have some fun with images, but make sure they're relevant to your brand and speak to your target market.
    Don’t forget about layout
    Many brands believe that once they have their logo, color scheme, and fonts, they are ready to go, but this is not the case. It's also crucial to consider how those elements interact. Your ability to present them as part of a cohesive strategy can make or break your brand recognition.
    Use the visuals to bring out emotions
    The stronger your emotional connection with your audience, the more likely they are to trust you and eventually purchase from you. When people come across your brand, what do you want them to think and feel? Consider these questions, and keep in mind that visuals can tell customers whether or not your brand is a good fit for them faster than words can.
    Test your initial concepts
    You should test and optimize your visual elements, just like any other marketing element, until you find one that truly resonates with your ideal customers. Document your style guide within your company's processes once you've finalized the visual component. If you ever need to hire new designers, this will ensure that nothing is overlooked as you move forward. Always remember to put strategy first in all aspects of marketing. It is the foundation of everything you do. If you can get that in place, it will be much easier to create the visual elements because you will have a clear understanding of who your company is, what it represents, and who you want to attract.
